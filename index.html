Photoshop Tutorial — Creative Text & Glitch Exploration

Purpose: This exercise helps you use text in a creative way and explore how words can show meaning visually. You will choose a few important words or short phrases from your artist or project research and turn them into image‑based text in Photoshop. This task encourages careful reading, creative thinking, and visual experimentation. It is especially useful for international and foundation‑level students who are learning how to connect research writing with practical design ideas. You will also learn simple Photoshop glitch techniques that show feeling or interruption through controlled distortion.

Recommended setup: A3 landscape (420 × 297 mm) at 300 dpi

Sample Image of the Glitched Experiment

Step 1 – Select Key Words

  • Choose a handful of words or short phrases from your chosen text or artist reference.
  • Open a new Photoshop document (A3 landscape, 300 dpi).
  • Use the Type Tool (T) to type out your words.
  • Try minimal, structured fonts like Andale Mono or Orator Std.

Step 2 – Rasterize and Duplicate

  • In the Layers panel, right‑click → Rasterize Type.
  • Duplicate the rasterized layer (Cmd / Ctrl+J) to keep a backup.

Step 3 – Create Glitches

  • Use the Rectangular Marquee Tool to select thin horizontal strips.
  • Press Cmd / Ctrl+J to copy each strip onto new layers.
  • Use Move Tool (V) to shift strips slightly to add subtle displacement.

Step 4 – Wave Distortion

  • Select the strips and Merge Layers (Cmd/Ctrl+E).
  • Go to Filter → Distort → Wave for controlled ripple effects.
  • Add Noise for texture variation if you wish.

Step 5 – Integrate with Layout

  • Position the glitch text within your research page — as a background element or feature component.
  • Ensure it aligns with your artist study or research theme.
  • Keep the outcome meaningful and reflective.

Step 6 – Save and Reflect

  • Save your Photoshop file as a .PSD so that all layers stay editable. This means you can change or improve your design later.
  • To share your work on Padlet or use it as evidence in class, also save a copy as a .JPG file.
  • When you upload the image, add a short comment or reflection under it. You can describe what you learned, which parts were successful, or what you might try next time.
